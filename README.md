# Superstore Sales Analysis 

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the **Superstore retail dataset** to uncover insights about sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, and geographic trends.

Using **Power BI dashboards**, the analysis transforms raw transactional data into actionable insights that help understand:

- Overall sales performance
- Product category performance
- Customer profitability
- Geographic sales distribution

The goal of this project is to demonstrate how **data visualization and business intelligence techniques** can support better business decisions.

---

# Problem Statement

Retail companies generate large amounts of sales data, but extracting meaningful insights from that data can be challenging.

Without proper analysis, businesses struggle to answer questions such as:

- Which products generate the most revenue and profit?
- Which customers are the most valuable?
- Which regions and states drive the most sales?
- Are high sales always associated with high profitability?

This project aims to analyze these questions using **interactive dashboards and exploratory data analysis**.

---

# Dataset

The analysis uses the **Superstore dataset**, a retail dataset containing transactional sales data from a fictional office supply company.

This dataset is widely used for **data analysis, business intelligence, and visualization practice**.

### Dataset Features

The dataset includes the following attributes:

- Order Date
- Sales
- Profit
- Discount
- Region
- State
- City
- Customer Name
- Segment
- Product Category
- Sub-Category
- Product Name

# Geography Analysis

This dashboard analyzes **sales performance across regions, states, and cities** to understand geographic revenue distribution and identify high-performing markets.

### Sales by Region

- West (~31.6%) generates the highest share of total sales.
- East (~29.6%) follows closely as the second largest market.
- Central (~21.8%) contributes a moderate portion of revenue.
- South (~17%) records the lowest sales among the regions.

### Sales by City Over Time

Key observations:

- Cities such as **New York** and **Los Angeles** consistently generate strong revenue.
- Sales levels vary between years, showing fluctuations in demand across different locations.
- Some cities experience noticeable growth during specific periods.

### Average Discount by State

- States like **Texas** and **Illinois** tend to apply higher average discounts.
- Other states maintain lower discount levels while still achieving strong sales.
- Discount strategies appear to vary depending on regional market conditions.

### Sales and Profit by State

- **California** generates the highest sales and profit.
- **New York** ranks second in revenue contribution.
- Some states show **high sales but relatively lower profit**, suggesting possible impacts from discounting or lower product margins.

### Insights

- The **West region is the strongest market in terms of revenue**.
- Major metropolitan areas contribute significantly to total sales.
- Discount strategies differ across states and may affect profitability.
- High sales in some states do not always translate into high profit.
---

# Key Insights

From the overall analysis:

- The **Technology category generates the largest revenue share**.
- Some high-revenue customers produce **negative profit due to discounts**.
- The **Consumer segment drives the majority of sales and profit**.
- The **West region is the strongest geographic market**.
- **California is the most profitable state**.
- Discount strategies vary significantly across states.
